Chinese Fishing Nets

Fort Kochi, Ernakulam

Overview

The Chinese Fishing Nets, locally known as *Cheena Vala*, are iconic structures lining the shores of Fort Kochi. Introduced by Chinese explorers in the 14th century, these massive cantilevered fishing nets offer a fascinating view into the historic and cultural exchanges that shaped Kochi’s past. They are not just functional but also a popular tourist attraction, especially during sunset.

Best Time to Visit

October to March

Timings

6:00 AM – 7:00 PM

Entry Fee

Free

History & Travel Info

These fishing nets are believed to have been brought to Kochi by traders from the court of Kublai Khan. Operated by a team of fishermen, the unique mechanism uses large stones as counterweights. Visitors can observe the traditional method of fishing and even try their hand at it for a small fee. The area also has street food vendors and local handicraft shops.

  • Nearest Railway: Ernakulam Junction (13 km)
  • Nearest Airport: Cochin International Airport (45 km)
  • Nearby Attractions: Fort Kochi Beach, St. Francis Church, Mattancherry Palace
