Edakkal Caves

Wayanad, Kerala

Overview

Edakkal Caves, nestled in the Ambukuthi Hills of Wayanad, are renowned for their ancient petroglyphs dating back to the Neolithic era. These rock carvings provide valuable insights into prehistoric human life and are a significant archaeological treasure in South India.

Best Time to Visit

October to March

Timings

9:00 AM – 4:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)

Entry Fee

₹50 for Adults, ₹20 for Children

History & Travel Info

Discovered in 1890 by Fred Fawcett, a British police officer, the Edakkal Caves house petroglyphs believed to be over 8,000 years old. The carvings depict human and animal figures, tools, and symbols, indicating the presence of a prehistoric civilization. The trek to the caves includes a steep climb and offers scenic views of the Wayanad hills.

  • Nearest Railway: Kozhikode Railway Station (about 97 km)
  • Nearest Airport: Calicut International Airport (about 106 km)
  • Nearby Attractions: Wayanad Heritage Museum, Soochipara Falls
