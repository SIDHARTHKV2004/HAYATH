Pallippuram Fort

Vypin Island, Ernakulam

Overview

Pallippuram Fort, also known as Paleport or Castelo em Cima, is one of the oldest surviving European monuments in India. Built by the Portuguese in 1503, this hexagonal-shaped fort was strategically located on the northern tip of Vypin Island to guard the entrance to the Periyar River. The fort stands as a testament to Kerala’s colonial history and maritime trade.

Best Time to Visit

October to March

Visiting Hours

9:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Entry Fee

Free

History & Travel Info

Originally built by the Portuguese in 1503 and later captured by the Dutch in the 17th century, Pallippuram Fort played a key role in the European colonial rivalry in Kerala. The fort features thick laterite walls, small openings for cannons, and a dungeon used in ancient times. Though not in active use today, it’s maintained as a protected monument under the Archaeological Survey of India.
