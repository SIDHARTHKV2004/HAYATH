Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Wayanad, Kerala

Overview

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, nestled in the Western Ghats, is Kerala's second-largest wildlife sanctuary. It forms a part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and is home to elephants, tigers, leopards, and rare bird species, making it a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Best Time to Visit

October to May

Timings

7:00 AM – 10:00 AM, 3:00 PM – 5:00 PM

Entry Fee

₹20 (Indian nationals), ₹110 (foreigners) + extra for vehicle and camera

History & Travel Info

Established in 1973, the sanctuary covers an area of over 340 sq. km and is divided into four ranges: Sulthan Bathery, Muthanga, Kurichiat, and Tholpetty. It offers guided safari tours, making it an ideal destination to spot wild elephants and learn about the rich flora and fauna of the region.

  • Nearest Railway: Kozhikode Railway Station (around 100 km)
  • Nearest Airport: Calicut International Airport (about 120 km)
  • Nearby Attractions: Edakkal Caves, Tholpetty Forest, Kuruva Island
