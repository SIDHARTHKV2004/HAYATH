Bolgatty Palace

Ernakulam, Kerala

Overview

Bolgatty Palace is a historic Dutch palace located on Bolgatty Island in Kochi, Ernakulam district. Built in 1744 by Dutch traders, it is one of the oldest existing Dutch palaces outside Holland. Now converted into a heritage hotel, the palace combines colonial architecture with modern amenities, making it a luxurious getaway.

Best Time to Visit

October to March

Visiting Hours

9:30 AM – 5:00 PM

Entry Fee

₹20 for Adults, ₹10 for Children

History & Travel Info

Originally built as a mansion by the Dutch, Bolgatty Palace was later taken over by the British and served as the residence of the British governors. The building is surrounded by gardens, backwaters, and a golf course, offering a regal experience. Today, it operates under the Kerala Tourism Development Corporation (KTDC) as a premium heritage resort.

  • Nearest Railway: Ernakulam Junction (5 km)
  • Nearest Airport: Cochin International Airport (35 km)
  • Nearby Attractions: Marine Drive, Fort Kochi, Mattancherry Palace
