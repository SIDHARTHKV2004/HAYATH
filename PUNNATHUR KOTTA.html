Punnathur Kotta

Guruvayur, Kerala

Overview

Punnathur Kotta, also known as the Elephant Fort, is a sanctuary for the elephants that belong to the Guruvayur Temple. Located about 3 km from the temple town of Guruvayur, it is home to more than 50 elephants. These majestic creatures are trained and cared for in this historic fort-like setting, offering a unique experience for visitors.

Best Time to Visit

October to March

Visiting Hours

9:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Entry Fee

₹10 (Adults), ₹5 (Children)

History & Travel Info

The property was once the palace of a local ruler and has since been converted into an elephant sanctuary. It is one of the very few places in the world where visitors can observe temple elephants up close. These elephants play a vital role in temple rituals and festivals, especially at Guruvayur Temple. Photography is restricted in certain areas.

  • Nearest Railway: Guruvayur Railway Station (3 km)
  • Nearest Airport: Cochin International Airport (80 km)
  • Nearby Attractions: Guruvayur Temple, Mammiyoor Temple, Chavakkad Beach
