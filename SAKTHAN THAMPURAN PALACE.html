Sakthan Thampuran Palace

Thrissur, Kerala

Overview

Sakthan Thampuran Palace, located in the heart of Thrissur city, is a historic landmark named after the revered King Rama Varma Sakthan Thampuran. The palace is known for its unique Kerala-Dutch style architecture and beautifully landscaped gardens. It now functions as a museum showcasing the cultural and historical legacy of Kerala.

Best Time to Visit

November to February

Visiting Hours

9:30 AM – 4:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)

Entry Fee

₹20 (Adults), ₹10 (Children)

History & Travel Info

Built in the 18th century, the palace is closely linked to Sakthan Thampuran, the architect of modern Thrissur. It includes a heritage museum, bronze gallery, ancient manuscripts, coins, and relics from the era of the Cochin royal family. The palace’s serene atmosphere and heritage charm make it a must-visit for history lovers.

  • Nearest Railway: Thrissur Railway Station (2.5 km)
  • Nearest Airport: Cochin International Airport (50 km)
  • Nearby Attractions: Vadakkumnathan Temple, Thrissur Zoo, Bible Tower
